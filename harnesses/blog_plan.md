3. **Energy Consumption at Mines** — energy use varies by mineral type, depth, equipment; underground vs open-pit; precious vs base metal. Four reasons to understand energy needs: cost savings, environmental sustainability, regulatory compliance, operational efficiency. Closes promoting Woven Mining's data platform for cross-site energy comparison.
4. **A Primer on Alt Data in Mining** — information asymmetry in mining analysis; traditional financials are incomplete. Three alt-data sources: Satellite Imagery, Social Media Activity, Weather Patterns. Woven Mining's angle: pairing alt data with technical/financial document access. Closes with a call to action for analysts covering public miners.

- **Palette/tokens**: `--bg`, `--bg2`, `--bg3`, `--accent`, `--accent-light`, `--white`, `--off-white`, `--muted`, `--border` (warm dark earthy theme).
- **Shared chrome**: `nav.site-nav` + hamburger `.mobile-menu` (same markup as other pages, add `aria-current="page"` on the Blog link once it has a real href), and the standard `<footer>` block.
- **Utilities**: `.container`, `.section-label`, `.section-title`, `.section-sub`, `.btn` / `.btn-primary` / `.btn-outline`, `.fade-up` (+ `-delay-1/2/3`) scroll-reveal classes.

- **Card pattern** (from services.html `.service-detail-card` / about.html `.team-card`): rounded `--bg2` card, 1px `--border`, `core-top` drill-core color-segment strip, `card-body` padding, hover lift + shadow. Reuse this as the **post card** on the listing page.
- Each page currently keeps its bespoke CSS in a `<style>` block in `<head>` rather than a separate stylesheet — follow that convention for `blog/index.html` and the post template's unique bits (e.g., article typography), keeping genuinely shared rules in `css/styles.css` only if reused 3+ times.
